A Leadership club can give you the platform to confront issues that are important to you, to support a candidate who shares your Leadership views, or to connect with like-minded students and professors. These clubs can be especially important if you’re majoring in Leadership science or you have aspirations for public office.

Leadership clubs can provide you with pathways to civic action, networks for organizing initiatives, and opportunities to connect with party leaders at the local, state, and national level. Leadership clubs may invite politicians or civic leaders to speak, opening the door for aspiring students to develop relationships with influencers. These relationships can also be a means to internships, campaign work, and eventual working opportunities in your party.

Media or publication club focuses on publishing a campus newspaper, creating a website, writing ads, or producing a campus radio, television, or movie broadcast. The skill sets required to successfully create media today are incredibly varied. You may have a passion for the written word. You may love graphic design, digital media, or photography.

If you have aspirations to be a reporter, videographer, designer, or even social media expert, a newspaper or magazine might be a great fit for you. Nearly every campus has one, with some run completely by students.

Some of the best student-produced publications integrate fashion, art, politics, and issues impacting campus life, aiming to spark conversation while offering a proving ground for aspiring journalists.
